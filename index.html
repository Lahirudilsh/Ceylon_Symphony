Problem

Research Problem

Technology is a versatile component in every industry in the modern world. But Sri Lanka is still behind when compared to the rest of the world. Especially in the Sri Lankan Music industry, technological usage is at a minimum level. Because of that, native musical innovations are rarely developed. As we declared earlier the rest of the world gets benefits from the ease of access to musical innovations. When a musician or anyone interested in playing a musical instrument who is unable to physically practice or study mostly uses a tool that includes virtual functionality from the various available options.[1] As an example, when an individual lacks access to actual drums but needs to create a beat, they can utilize a tool such as a virtual drum set. Sri Lankan musical culture has vastly its native percussion instruments such as Thammattama, Rabana, Geta Bera, Yak Bera, etc. However, Sri Lanka does not possess any tools that allow the virtual play of these instruments, as previously mentioned. If there is a need to play these instruments, the only option is to physically play the instrument[2]. As part of this research, we aim to create a solution for that matter. Also, music transcribing refers to the process of converting audio or live musical performances into written musical notations[3]. It is a manual process that is mostly done by a skilled transcriber. There are software programs available that can assist with the process by analyzing the audio and generating a transcription automatically to western Notation. As a problem there isnâ€™t any software or tool to do that process in eastern notation[4]. As we identified earlier in Sri Lanka need for this type of tool is priceless. The majority of the most renowned musical compositions and songs are only available in Western notations [5]. However, the majority of Sri Lankans cannot read Western notations. The acquisition of this skill can be a time-consuming process, and for some individuals, it may not be a priority. This leads to the requirement for conversion of the notations to an Eastern format. In such situations, it is always necessary to have a person with the knowledge to convert Western notations to Eastern notations. Regrettably, individuals with this capability are rare to find. The lack of ability to read Western notations among the majority of Sri Lankans and the scarcity of individuals with the knowledge to convert Western notations to Eastern notations pose significant challenges for those who wish to play or learn traditional music from the country. As part of this research, we create a notation translation as the solution for this problem. In conclusion, the lack of technology in the Sri Lankan music industry has resulted in limited access to musical innovations and a lack of native musical developments. The lack of virtual tools for the traditional instruments and the difficulty in transcribing and translating musical notations have resulted in significant challenges for those who wish to play or learn traditional music from the country. The aim of this research is to address these challenges by creating a virtual tool for the native instruments, a tool for transcribing music into Eastern notations, and a notation translation system. This research will not only help to preserve and promote the rich musical culture of Sri Lanka but also make it accessible to a wider audience.

Solution

Research Solution

The solution to the problems described in the research document is to create a virtual tool for the traditional musical instruments in Sri Lanka including, Virtual tool for Sri Lankan percussion musical instruments: The virtual tool would enable musicians and individuals interested in playing traditional instruments to practice & study without access to physical instruments. The tool would include virtual functionality and allow users to play and create music with native instruments such as Thammattama, Rabana, Geta Bera, Yak Bera, etc. Traditional Percussion Instrument Sample Library with sample wave slicing tool: Beat detection to slice a wave file into pieces and make them independently playable from the Piano roll or a controller & Creating a Traditional percussion Instrument Music library with proper optimization. The transcribing tool would assist with the process of converting audio or live musical performances into written musical notations in Eastern format. The tool would analyze the audio and generate a transcription automatically, making it easier and more accessible for individuals who wish to play or learn traditional music from Sri Lanka. Notation translation system: The notation translation system would address the challenge of converting Western notations to Eastern notations, making traditional music more accessible to a wider audience. This system would convert Western notations into Eastern notations, making it easier for those who are unable to read Western notations to understand and play traditional music. In conclusion, these tools help to preserve and promote the rich musical culture of Sri Lanka, making more accessible to everyone.
